SERVICES

Home / What We Do

Health

Multiple health camps were conducted to identify children’s health problems and link the children to the appropriate services. The camps included: general health check-ups to identify chronic illness, screenings for adolescent girls, dental screenings, and ophthalmological disorder screenings.

Education

5,722 children of KP have received highly needed education services, including educational scholarships through the Integrated Child Protection Scheme (ICPS) and educational supplies (school bags and books) through private and corporate support. RIDES also mobilized support from local business entities to ensure access to education supplies for children.

Nutrition

We provide nutrition-related services. While the efforts to develop formal linkages to the government run Integrated Child Development Scheme are happening in all states, much of the support in this quarter has been from local donors and community groups. In Andhra Pradesh. The Rice Millers Associations are key stakeholders in the private sector, and the project has developed linkage in East Godavari district to provide rations to the OVC families on a regular basis.

Life-Skills Education

Adolescent children (10-18 years) of were engaged and provided life-skills education that enables them to make informed choices and avoid exposure to behaviours that makes them vulnerable to acquiring HIV. Overall, 6,798 children have been enrolled in life-skills education programs, at which they are provided life-skills education that enhance their ability to deal with the vulnerabilities they face due to being children of KP.

Social Security

The project also provided services to 686 care givers which included parenting education, based on the Care givers Toolkit, to 616 care givers. 117 caregivers were linked to livelihood related services while 154 received social security services. Some of the key activities undertaken during this quarter included: providing access to social protection programs for caregivers children adolescents. This is done through linkage with several corporations, both in Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra, including ONGC, Amazon, DDB Mudra, and local companies.

HIV Testing & Treatment

The primary focus of the project has been prioritizing the children of HIV positive KPs – testing the never tested children and adolescents manifesting risk behaviour. During last year, 666 children were found to be eligible for HIV testing based on the positive HIV status of their parents, of which 28 children knew their HIV status. Index testing of children was initiated for remaining 494 children with 21 knew HIV positive children identified with a case detection rate of 4.3 percent. 39 of the 49 HIV positive children (21 newly identified and 28 known positive children) are initiated on treatment (80%).
